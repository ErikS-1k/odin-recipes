Milanesa

Description

Milanesas are a classic staple of the Argentine table. Their origins trace back to the cotoletta alla milanesa from northern Italy, but in Argentina they took on a life of their own

Here you'll find the classic beef version, but also chicken, pork, and even vegetable varieties like eggplant or zucchini.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg of thinly sliced beef (round or topside cut)
  • 3 eggs
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, to taste
  • Salt and pepper
  • Breadcrumbs, as needed
  • Flour, as needed
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  1. In a bowl, beat the eggs with the minced garlic, parsley, salt and pepper. Mix well and set aside.
  2. Coat the beef slices first in flour, shaking off the excess, then dip in the egg mixture, and finally coat in breadcrumbs, pressing firmly so the breading sticks well.
  3. Place the breaded milanesas on a tray and let them rest in the fridge for at least 30 minutes before frying.
  4. Heat plenty of oil in a pan and fry the milanesas until golden on both sides. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  5. Serve hot, with french fries, mashed potatoes, or salad. They can also be baked in the oven for a lighter version.
